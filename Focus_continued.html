What is focusing? What is a focused life?

Intro

As a high schooler navigating the complexities of modern life, I find myself at a crossroads—a place where the expectations of my generation and the realities of our digital existence intersect in ways that are both overwhelming and unsettling. Being part of Western Gen-Z, society expects us to live a life steeped in digital interaction—sharing every moment on Instagram, getting news from YouTube, entertaining ourselves on TikTok, buying everything from Amazon, and sinking countless hours into the latest video games. You get the point; it's how most of us live. Yet, how often do we stop to think about the consequences of such a lifestyle? Rarely, if ever. And it's not just a personal choice; it's an expectation, a societal norm reinforced by the fact that everyone around us, from friends to family, is digitally connected. We are expected to do the same, and the pressure to conform can be suffocating.

Why We Act Without Thinking

More often than not, we act without considering the long-term consequences of our actions. We're driven by our emotional desires, slaves to the whims of our ever-changing moods. We forgo long-term thinking in favor of instant gratification, constantly chasing the next dopamine hit, whether it’s the latest product, a new social media trend, or the newest release of something we didn’t even know we wanted until five minutes ago. This masochistic cycle defines the lifestyle of nearly every Gen-Z individual. While it's human nature to desire, the problem arises when these desires are mistaken for what truly brings value to our lives. So, the question becomes—what does bring value? In our shallow, surface-level existences, this is a rare discussion, one that seems to have been lost between generations.

The 'Why'

Gen-Z—the distracted generation, the vain generation, the connected generation, the indulgent generation—will soon be running society. As someone who is part of this generation, I can offer an insider's perspective, exploring why we live the way we do, why we’re expected to live this way, and how we ended up here in the first place. These questions are daunting, often sparking anxiety, especially for someone like me who is already prone to overthinking. But they’re important questions, ones that we have to confront if we ever hope to adjust our lives for the better. In my opinion, there are a few key reasons why we've ended up like this.

Chasing Value

Chasing value is nothing new for humans; it’s something we’ve done for as long as we’ve existed. But meaning—true, deep meaning—is something that Gen-Z is sorely lacking. We’ve been raised in comfort, pampered to the point where we don’t understand suffering or hardship in the way previous generations did. Our lives have been so easy that we’ve become uncomfortable with any sort of pain. What’s unique about our generation is our unprecedented access to distraction. Nihilism, or the belief that life is inherently meaningless, seems to be the underlying philosophy driving our lifestyles. As religion and traditional moral values become increasingly unpopular, the typical goals and motivations that drove our predecessors have been lost. Without a guiding light, it’s difficult to know what we should be chasing after—especially when, according to nihilism, there’s no intrinsic meaning to life anyway.

The Search—Finding a Balanced Lifestyle

Is it possible to live in this hyper-connected world while still choosing a focused, meaningful lifestyle? These two seemingly opposing lifestyles—one driven by technology, the other by a desire for depth and focus—seem to clash, each valuing different ideals. Should we live like Luddites, rejecting technology and returning to a simpler time? Or should we continue as we are, constantly connected, constantly stimulated, but perhaps constantly shallow as well?

A Wholesome Existence

The virtuous life is often romanticized—a life of simplicity, focus, and intention. It’s about finding meaning in the small moments, cultivating deep relationships, and pursuing passions that bring genuine fulfillment. But in a world that’s constantly pulling us in a million different directions, the idea of living virtuously can feel almost impossible. We’re bombarded with information, distractions, and pressures to conform. We’re told that success looks like having the latest gadget, the most followers, the most likes. Yet, deep down, many of us feel a sense of emptiness, a nagging feeling that we’re missing out on something more substantial.

So, what does a wholesome existence look like for Gen-Z? It might mean setting boundaries with our technology, carving out time for reflection, and prioritizing relationships over online validation. It could involve seeking out experiences that challenge us, that push us out of our comfort zones, rather than settling for the easy, predictable paths laid out for us. A wholesome life isn’t about rejecting technology outright, but about using it in ways that enhance our lives rather than detract from them. It’s about being intentional with our time, our attention, and our energy—choosing to invest in things that bring true value, rather than fleeting satisfaction.

The Balance Between Connection and Isolation

Finding balance is key. In a world where we’re more connected than ever, it’s ironic that so many of us feel isolated, lonely, and disconnected from ourselves and others. The constant noise of social media, the endless scrolling, and the pressure to always be "on" can leave us feeling burnt out and emotionally drained. Yet, the solution isn’t necessarily to unplug completely. After all, technology does offer real benefits—connecting us with loved ones, providing access to information, and offering platforms for self-expression and creativity.

The challenge lies in finding a way to harness these benefits without letting them take over our lives. This might mean setting limits on our screen time, being mindful of how we engage with social media, and making space for offline activities that nourish our souls. It’s about finding that sweet spot where we can enjoy the advantages of a connected world while still maintaining our mental and emotional well-being. It’s not about rejecting the digital world entirely, but about learning to navigate it with a sense of purpose and mindfulness.

Concluding Thoughts—Who Is This For?

While this piece advocates for the virtues of a focused life, it would be a disservice not to consider the genuine reasons that might contradict the arguments I’ve made. After all, the truth is rarely simple, and living a meaningful life in the digital age is more complex than any single perspective can capture.
